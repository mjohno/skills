# Retro Checklist

Use this checklist after validating a completed STEP and before recording its `retro`.

## Purpose

A retro is an evidence-based reflection on whether the completed step moved the workflow closer to its goal.

## Checklist

- [ ] Use the recorded work and validation evidence to judge whether the step moved the workflow closer to its goal; do not restate that judgment as a win.
- [ ] Record `wins` only for surprising, reusable, or otherwise useful observations about what worked well, including what proved effective when validation was `partial`. Leave `wins` empty when it merely restates the work, validation result, or evidence.
- [ ] Record `issues`: friction, failures, uncertainty, or blockers that impeded progress.
- [ ] Record `actions`: proposed changes that reduce friction, improve success rates, or remove blockers.
- [ ] Keep `actions` independent: an action may become a durable lesson, a next-step candidate, both, or neither.
- [ ] Record durable learning in `lessons` and proposed work in `next_steps`; do not treat either as a required outcome of every action.

`validate` records whether the current step met its intent and criteria. Retro captures useful observations and improvement opportunities; it does not duplicate the work summary, validation result, or evidence.
