# Spec Quality Criteria

Use this for review. Quality is about clarity, traceability, and future-state usefulness.

## Review Questions

1. Does the spec explain why it exists without prematurely planning implementation?
2. Are current-state claims separated from assumptions?
3. Is the future state concrete enough for a plan to target?
4. Are in-scope and out-of-scope boundaries clear?
5. Are requirements testable, reviewable, or otherwise judgeable?
6. Does acceptance define observable judgment signals?
7. Are constraints separated from priorities?
8. Are risks, questions, assumptions, and pre-work visibly separated?
9. Are settled decisions distinct from assumptions and open questions?
10. Can downstream work trace to stable IDs without copying large sections?

## Common Findings

- Future state describes activity instead of end condition.
- Requirements duplicate scope or acceptance.
- Acceptance mandates implementation artifacts too early.
- Uncertainties are hidden as requirements.
- IDs are missing, unstable, or overused on every sentence.
