# Knowledge Quality Criteria

Use this for review of MKF concepts, bundles, or knowledge-interface consumers.

## Review Questions

1. Is the concept title clear and specific?
2. Does the description summarize the concept in one useful line?
3. Are tags useful for retrieval without becoming noisy?
4. Is the concept body focused on one durable idea or reusable artifact?
5. Are citations present where claims need attribution?
6. Are generated index concepts clearly separated from authored concepts?
7. Can manual bundle discovery be understood from visible environment/path information?
8. Are lookup/search behaviors kept out of passive knowledge contract material?
9. Are write/update/index-rebuild behaviors kept in record tooling?

## Common Findings

- Concept mixes unrelated ideas.
- Description is too vague to help lookup results.
- Tags duplicate words from the title without improving retrieval.
- Operational behavior leaks into the interface contract.
- Bundle selector ambiguity is not reported clearly.
